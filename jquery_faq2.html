Do You Know Your National Parks? Take This Quiz!

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone National Park is named after the Yellowstone River, the major river running through the park.
  • Yellowstone is in the top five national parks for number of recreational visitors.
  • Approximately 738 people work for the National Park Service during the peak summer season.
  • Yellowstone encompasses 3,472 square miles (2,221,766 acres) which makes it larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined.
  • Eagle Peak in the southeastern part of Yellowstone is the highest at 11,358 feet.

Zion National Park

  • Before it was called Zion, the park was called "Mukuntuweap National Monument".
  • Zion is also home to the Kolob Arch, which is one of the largest in the world.
  • Inside of the Kolob Canyon District is the Kolob Volcano, a volcano more than 1.1 million years old.
  • Zion National Park’s geology features some of the tallest sandstone monoliths in the world.
  • Zion is home to the California Condor, which is the largest bird in North America.

Glacier National Park

  • The park’s water flows in three directions
  • The park has 734 miles of hiking trails
  • The park gets around 160 inches of snowfall annually.
  • The park’s nickname is the Crown of the Continent.
  • The land that now comprises Glacier previously belonged to the Blackfeet tribe.
